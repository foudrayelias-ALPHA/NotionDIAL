A model does not keep a concept as a word on a shelf; it holds it as a shape, arranged in the space of its own activations. Each figure below is one such shape, the days of the week, as a working model actually carries them. Drag the slider to turn a single dial in that model's weights: the shape dissolves as the concept is stripped away, then reassembles the moment you turn back. Five models answer the same dial, from 124M to 7B parameters, and the buttons choose which concept is put to the test.

At λ = 1 the model is untouched. Drag toward 0 to strip the concept away; push past 1 to overstate it. Every stop on the dial is a measurement, not an animation: what moves between them is the drawing, never the data.

Numbers, read first. While days and months yield to a clean cut, numbers do not: in all five models, removing them also disturbs the model's ordinary prose, because number words run through nearly everything a model does. The geometry still collapses, but the cost is spread wider, as the neighbor readouts below show.

How to read a panel

The solid, colored shape is the concept under the real edit; drag toward λ=0 and it folds inward to a single point. The dashed shape is the placebo. Beneath each panel stand three measures: the target concept, which should drain toward empty; the model's task on that concept, which follows it down; and a neighbor concept riding the very same edited weights, which should hold near full. That last bar is the crux. A cut is only specific if the thing beside it survives. The small figure is the exact value, and ctrl marks the placebo. Each panel is scaled to its own 26 frames, so motion within a panel is honestly comparable.

What the sweep shows

For days and for months, the edit takes the target concept and leaves its neighbors, and the model's wider behavior, intact. The months are the instructive case: their shape collapses in every model, yet only some models actually forget how to use them. Whether a concept truly stands on a given manifold, then, is decided by the particular wiring of each model, not by its size. Numbers collapse as well, though never cleanly. Where a preregistered test fails, the paper records it rather than burying it; a result worth trusting is one that reports its own exceptions.

Method & honesty

A single dial in weight space, W(λ) = W + (λ−1)·M, bends the one real manifold; λ moves across 26 preregistered doses on the grid [−0.5…1.5]. Every point is a measured forward pass, neither interpolated nor invented; only the positions of the dots glide between the frames we recorded. A random edit of equal size should barely disturb these low-dimensional views, so the case for specificity rests on the readouts, not on the placebo alone. The fuller atlas and the paper sit beside this demo in the repository, for anyone who would rather check than trust. Nothing on this page was drawn that was not first measured.
